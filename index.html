Summer Research Intern
Indian Institute of Science - Bengaluru, KA, India
May 2016 to July 2016

In the summer of 2016, I was 1 out of 4 undergraduate students selected for a summer research internship program in High Performance Computer Architecture (HPCA) at the Supercomputer Education and Research Center (SERC) located at the prestigious Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in Bengaluru, KA.

I worked with Dr. Matthew Jacob Thazhuthaveetil's team to identify similar sequences in the execution flow of large-scale programs like the GNU GCC compiler. We used data points like cache hits/misses counters, instructions-per-cycle (IPC) counters in discretized blocks of the total instruction trace. The data points were then fed to a regression tree algorithm to identify clusters of code which had similar data points. The clusters can then be utilized with compiler optimization techniques to improve resource utilization of the computer.

Alongside the research internship, I also participated in a summer school program to learn about advanced computer architecture and GPGPU programming using different languages like NVIDIA CUDA, OpenMP and MPI. We also got a chance to use some cool tools like DRAMSim and SimpleScalar which made the job of analyzing the behavior of computer microarchitecture much easier for me.
